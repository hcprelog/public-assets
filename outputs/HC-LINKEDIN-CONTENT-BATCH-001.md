## POST 2 — Education / Controversy

Most small businesses are chasing 8(a) status when they should be going for SDVOSB.

Here's why.

The 8(a) program:
- 9-year term (starts over if you leave)
- SBA approval required for every contract over $25K
- Graduation means you lose the benefit entirely
- Extremely competitive to get into

SDVOSB:
- No term limit — you keep it as long as you qualify
- Self-certify through SBA (faster)
- Any VA contract can be set aside exclusively for you
- VA is one of the largest federal buyers in the country

If you're a service-disabled veteran, SDVOSB is your fastest path to a federal award.

If you can stack SDVOSB + HUBZone — you have two set-aside pools to fish from.
That's the play we run at H&C.

Are you SDVOSB certified? Have you looked at what VA is buying in your NAICS code this month?

## POST 4 — Transparency / Process

What a real federal bid looks like from the inside.

Most people think winning government contracts requires a massive team and a fancy proposal.

Here's the actual process for a purchase order under $250K:

**Day 1:** Find the solicitation on SAM.gov
**Day 2:** Read the entire Statement of Work (not just the summary)
**Day 3:** Run bid/no-bid: Do we have past perf? Are we competitive on price? Is the timeline realistic?
**Day 4–6:** Write the technical approach (respond directly to every requirement)
**Day 7:** Price it. Know your floor. Don't win a contract you can't execute.
**Day 8:** Proofread for compliance (are all required docs attached?)
**Day 9:** Submit before the deadline — not the morning of

The difference between firms that win and firms that don't isn't proposal writing talent.

It's consistency. Submit 2 bids per month, every month. At a 20% win rate, you win 5 contracts per year.

## POST 5 — Authority / GSA

If you're a small business doing any federal consulting work, the absence of a GSA Schedule is costing you contracts you don't even know you're losing.

Here's why:

Federal contracting officers love the GSA Schedule because:
→ They don't have to run a full competition
→ Pricing is pre-negotiated (they know they're getting fair market value)
→ They can award in days, not months
→ Compliance is simplified

If you're not on GSA MAS, contracting officers can't easily buy from you even if they want to.

We're in the process of applying right now under SIN 541614 (logistics consulting).

The application takes 3–6 months. That means if you start today, you could be on schedule by end of year.

Required: 2 years in business + past performance + financial statements.
